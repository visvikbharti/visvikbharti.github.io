Development of an In Silico Platform (TRIPinRNA) for the Identification of Novel RNA Intramolecular Triple Helices and Their Validation Using Biophysical Techniques

Isha Rakheja*, Vishal Bharti*, et al. (* Equal contribution)

Biochemistry (2024) DOI: 10.1021/acs.biochem.4c00334

This paper introduces TRIPinRNA, a computational tool we developed to predict intramolecular RNA triple helix structures. These structures play critical roles in gene regulation, particularly in X chromosome inactivation. We validated our predictions using several biophysical techniques, demonstrating the accuracy and utility of our approach.

An Interface of Genetically Engineered Human Forebrain Assembloids and Polymeric Nanofiber Scaffolds for Multiscale Profiling of Interneuron Migration Disorders

Rauthan, R., Bharti, V., et al.

Under revision @ EMBO Reports DOI: 10.21203/rs.3.rs-3831019/v1

This manuscript describes an innovative approach combining genetically engineered human forebrain assembloids with polymeric nanofiber scaffolds to study interneuron migration disorders. Our computational analyses of RNA-seq and protein-protein interaction networks provide insights into the molecular mechanisms underlying these disorders.

MLC1 alteration in iPSCs give rise to disease-like cellular vacuolation phenotype in the astrocyte lineage

Sharma, S., Bharti, V., Das, P.K., et al.

Preprint DOI: 10.1101/2025.01.06.631607

This study investigates how alterations in the MLC1 gene affect cellular vacuolation in astrocytes, providing insights into the mechanisms underlying megalencephalic leukoencephalopathy with subcortical cysts (MLC). Our computational analyses of RNA-seq data helped identify key pathways and genes involved in this rare neurological disorder.
