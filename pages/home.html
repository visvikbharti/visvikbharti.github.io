About Me

I am a dedicated Bioinformatician currently working as a Project Associate-II at the Institute of Genomics and Integrative Biology (CSIR-IGIB) in New Delhi. With a background in both electronics and biotechnology, I bring a unique interdisciplinary perspective to computational biology challenges.

My research focuses on RNA biology, single-cell genomics analysis, and the development of computational tools that bridge the gap between complex statistical techniques and biological interpretation. I'm particularly passionate about creating accessible tools for researchers with limited statistical backgrounds.

Growing up in a small town in Jharkhand as a first-generation engineer has instilled in me values of perseverance, curiosity, and a desire to make scientific tools more accessible to everyone. I believe in applying science and technology in ways that genuinely improve human health and expand our understanding of life.

I am currently working on various computational biology projects, with a particular focus on developing StickForStats, an advanced statistical analysis platform designed to make complex statistical methods accessible to researchers without extensive statistical knowledge.

Current Work

At IGIB, I am currently leading multiple projects:

  • TRIPinRNA: Development of a computational tool for predicting intramolecular RNA triple helix structures, with applications in X chromosome inactivation and gene regulation
  • StickForStats: Creating a comprehensive statistical analysis platform to enable researchers to perform advanced analyses without extensive statistical knowledge
  • Human Forebrain Assembloids: Performing computational analyses of RNA-seq data to understand interneuron migration disorders
  • DNA Repair Mechanism Analysis: Using multi-layer inference methods to decipher repair factor requirements in different DNA break scenarios
