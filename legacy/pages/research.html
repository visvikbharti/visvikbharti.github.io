Current Research Projects

Development of an In Silico Platform (TRIPinRNA) for the Identification of Novel RNA Intramolecular Triple Helices and Their Validation Using Biophysical Techniques

Role: Lead Developer | Supervisor: Dr. Debojyoti Chakraborty & Dr. Souvik Maiti

IGIB CSIR, New Delhi 2023-Present

Developed 'TRIPinRNA,' a Python-based tool for predicting intramolecular RNA triple helix structures, focusing on X chromosome inactivation and gene regulation. Our research identifies novel RNA structures that play crucial roles in gene expression and regulation, with potential implications for understanding genetic disorders.

Publication GitHub Repository

Deciphering DNA Repair Mechanisms through Multi-Layer Inference Analysis

Role: Lead Computational Analyst | Supervisor: Dr. Debojyoti Chakraborty

IGIB CSIR, New Delhi 2023-Present

Developed an innovative three-layer computational pipeline to decipher repair factor requirements in staggered versus blunt-end DNA breaks using scRNA-seq data. Implemented stringent QC workflow and optimized data integration approach (nFeature: 500-6500, nCount: 1000-45000). Identified and validated pathway-specific genes through multiple statistical layers (77 FZ+, 5 SZ+ unique genes; 23 FZ+/SZ-, 2 SZ+/FZ- overlaps).

Research Interests

RNA Biology G-quadruplexes Triple Helices Computational Genomics Single-cell Analysis Statistical Methods Machine Learning DNA Repair Tool Development Neurodevelopmental Disorders

My research is driven by a passion for understanding complex biological systems through computational approaches. I'm particularly interested in:

  • RNA Secondary and Tertiary Structures: Studying how non-canonical RNA structures like G-quadruplexes and triple helices influence gene regulation and cellular functions
  • Computational Genomics: Developing statistical and computational methods for analyzing complex genomic data
  • Single-cell Analysis: Applying advanced methods to analyze single-cell RNA-seq data to understand cellular heterogeneity
  • DNA Repair Mechanisms: Investigating the computational aspects of understanding NHEJ vs HDR pathways
  • Tool Development: Creating user-friendly computational tools to make advanced analyses accessible to researchers with limited computational backgrounds
  • Machine Learning in Genomics: Applying ML/DL techniques to solve biological problems

Future Research Directions

Looking ahead, I am interested in pursuing several research directions:

Integrated Multi-omics Frameworks

Developing computational frameworks that combine single-cell omics data with spatial information to gain comprehensive insights into cellular processes

Expanded RNA Structure Analysis

Expanding the TRIPinRNA platform to identify and analyze triple helices across different species and disease contexts

Accessible Statistical Tools

Creating more accessible statistical tools for researchers with diverse computational backgrounds to democratize advanced data analysis

RNA Structures in Neurological Disorders

Investigating the role of RNA secondary structures in neurological disorders to uncover potential therapeutic targets

Machine Learning for DNA Repair

Applying advanced machine learning approaches to predict DNA repair pathway choice in different cellular contexts
